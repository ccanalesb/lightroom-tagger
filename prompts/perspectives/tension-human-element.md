# Tension & Human Element

<!-- optional: true -->

Whether the photograph creates visible tension through people—their presence, gesture, activity, or interaction—or through clear human traces (movement, wear, personal objects, intervention) in the built environment. Tension means unresolved energy: something is happening, about to happen, or oddly out of place—not merely that a person exists in frame.

**Source anchor:** The author states that tension is essential in street photography and that boring photographs lack this human element.

## Scoring (1-10)

- **weak** (1-3): No people and no readable human traces; OR people present but inert—backs turned, anonymous crowd filler, static poses with no gesture or relationship to the scene. Nothing creates friction, curiosity, or narrative pressure; the human element is decorative or absent.
- **adequate** (4-6): Human presence or traces are visible but tension is mild: routine activity (walking, waiting, shopping) without a decisive gesture, contrast, or unresolved situation. A person anchors the frame but their role could be swapped for any passerby without changing the photograph's meaning.
- **strong** (7-10): Clear human-mediated tension: decisive gesture or expression, interaction between two or more figures, charged body language, ironic juxtaposition of person and environment, a figure caught mid-action at a narrative peak, or human traces (abandonment, disruption, personal detail) that raise an unanswered question about who was here and what occurred.
- **not_applicable** (5) (not attempted / not applicable): No human figure, shadow, silhouette, activity, or intentional human trace appears in the frame, and the image does not aim to read as a human story (e.g. pure geometry, weather, or nature with zero built-environment reference). Set `not_attempted` only when genuinely absent—not when people are present but weakly used.

# Intensity & Suggestion

Whether the photograph suggests meaning, feeling, or open questions beyond a literal caption—working like a short story rather than a inventory of what is visible. Intensity here is interpretive charge: the image makes the viewer feel something, doubt something, or construct a narrative the caption does not spell out.

**Source anchor:** Intensity is described as the "suggestion that the photograph makes" and is a core requirement for a photograph to work like a story.

## Scoring (1-10)

- **weak** (1-3): Pure description—the image reports what is in front of the camera with no ambiguity, metaphor, or emotional charge. One obvious reading; nothing lingers. Could be replaced by a one-sentence caption with no loss ("a man walks past a wall").
- **adequate** (4-6): Some mood, irony, or interest beyond the literal, but the suggested reading stays close to the surface—pleasant atmosphere, mild curiosity, or a single straightforward interpretation. The viewer understands the scene but is not prompted to rethink it or feel a strong pull.
- **strong** (7-10): The image generates suggestion: visual metaphor, timing, juxtaposition, omission, or ambiguity that opens multiple readings or a strong feeling (expectation, unease, tenderness, absurdity, doubt). Specific visible evidence—two elements in ironic relation, a gesture at peak tension, an unexplained detail—makes the photograph exceed description and reward interpretation.

# Interpretive & Human Charge

<!-- optional: true -->

Whether the photograph carries interpretive charge—suggesting meaning, feeling, or open questions beyond a literal caption—including charge that arises from the human element: gesture, expression, interaction, or human traces (movement, wear, personal objects, intervention) that create unresolved energy. The image should work like a short story rather than an inventory of what is visible: it makes the viewer feel something, doubt something, or construct a narrative the caption does not spell out.

**Source anchor:** Intensity is the "suggestion that the photograph makes" and is a core requirement for a photograph to work like a story; in street work that suggestion is often carried by human tension—something happening, about to happen, or oddly out of place—rather than a person merely being present.

## Scoring (1-10)

- **weak** (1-3): Pure description—the image reports what is in front of the camera with one obvious reading, no ambiguity, metaphor, or emotional charge; where people or human traces appear they are inert (backs turned, anonymous filler, static poses with no gesture or relationship). Nothing lingers. Could be replaced by a one-sentence caption with no loss ("a man walks past a wall").
- **adequate** (4-6): Some mood, irony, or interest beyond the literal, or human presence that anchors the frame, but the suggested reading stays close to the surface—pleasant atmosphere, mild curiosity, or routine activity (walking, waiting, shopping) without a decisive gesture or unresolved situation. The viewer understands the scene but is not prompted to rethink it or feel a strong pull.
- **strong** (7-10): The image generates suggestion: visual metaphor, timing, juxtaposition, omission, or ambiguity that opens multiple readings or a strong feeling (expectation, unease, tenderness, absurdity, doubt); OR clear human-mediated tension—a decisive gesture or expression, charged interaction between figures, ironic juxtaposition of person and environment, a figure caught mid-action at a narrative peak, or human traces that raise an unanswered question about who was here and what occurred. Cite the concrete visible evidence—two elements in ironic relation, a gesture at peak tension, an unexplained detail—that makes the photograph exceed description and reward interpretation.
- **not_applicable** (5) (not attempted / not applicable): Set `not_attempted` ONLY for pure abstraction, geometry, or nature with zero human presence AND no interpretive or narrative aim—an image where there is genuinely nothing to interpret. Do NOT use this band when the image merely reads flat or literal; a describable-but-inert photograph is weak (1-3), not excused.

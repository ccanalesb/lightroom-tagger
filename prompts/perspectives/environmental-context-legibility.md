# Depth & Environmental Context

<!-- optional: true -->

Whether the frame builds legible spatial depth and readable context: foreground, middleground, and background planes that each function and interact to create depth and narrative context, and an environment that stays sharp and readable enough to show place, spatial relationships, and parallel activity—not reduced to undifferentiated blur or flattened onto a single plane. This scores the *visible outcome* (what the viewer can read across the planes), not the aperture or technique used to make the image.

**Source anchor:** Layering works when foreground, middleground, and background each read well individually and enhance one another; and using an extremely open aperture "makes very little sense" when it fails to show the street and the interaction of things.

## Scoring (1-10)

- **weak** (1-3): Flat or collapsed—the subject sits on a single plane, or is isolated by shallow depth of field while background and midground collapse into color fields or unrecognizable shapes. The viewer cannot identify the type of place (shopfront, crossing, market aisle), count secondary figures, or see how the planes relate. The image could almost be a studio portrait dropped onto a generic blur.
- **adequate** (4-6): Some depth or context is visible—a general sense of place and one or two readable planes—but important midground or background detail is soft, cropped away, or too dark to parse. Place is identifiable in broad terms (e.g. "a city sidewalk") and planes coexist, but specific relationships, parallel events, or architectural cues that connect subject to environment are only partially legible and the planes do not clearly enhance one another.
- **strong** (7-10): Foreground, middleground, and background are each legible and interact: signage, architecture, other pedestrians, vehicles, or spatial depth remain sharp enough to show *interaction*—what else is happening near the subject and where they sit in the scene—and the planes reinforce one another to build depth and narrative context. Even selective focus keeps enough of the scene that the photograph shows the place, not just a figure cut out from it.
- **not_applicable** (5) (not attempted / not applicable): Set `not_attempted` ONLY when the image genuinely has no environment or depth to read—a deliberate flat studio or abstract frame with no place, no distinct planes, and no spatial context intended. Do NOT use this band when depth or context is attempted but poorly executed; a collapsed or blurred-out scene is weak (1-3), not excused.

# Compositional Cleanliness

Baseline compositional cleanliness: how free the frame is from distracting or competing elements, how controlled the use of negative space and edges is, and how clearly the primary subject reads. This is a baseline craft dimension that applies to EVERY photograph and can never be marked not_attempted.

**Source anchor:** A clean composition keeps the frame free of distractions so the subject reads clearly; this baseline craft applies to every photograph regardless of which optional techniques it uses.

## Scoring (1-10)

- **weak** (1-3): Cluttered or distracting composition: the frame is busy or chaotic, the primary subject competes with unmanaged background/foreground elements, negative space and edges are messy, and the eye is not clearly guided.
- **adequate** (4-6): Reasonably clean composition with some distracting elements or minor clutter that slightly dilute the subject or the reading of the frame.
- **strong** (7-10): Clean, intentional composition: the frame is free of distractions, negative space and edges are controlled, and the primary subject reads clearly and immediately.
- **not_applicable** (5) (not attempted / not applicable): Reserved band required by the schema. Compositional cleanliness is a baseline craft dimension that applies to every photograph; do NOT use this band — always score 1-10.

# Framing

<!-- optional: true -->

Evaluating the use of a natural framing device—foreground elements or structural features such as doorways, windows, arches, branches, railings, or shadows—arranged to encircle or enclose the primary subject. This assesses whether the device creates an appealing shape, integrates naturally into the environment, and directs focus toward the subject without becoming a distraction in its own right.

**Source anchor:** Framing at its most basic is using something within the frame to frame the subject you are interested in, or the thing people are interested in.

## Scoring (1-10)

- **weak** (1-3): No framing device, or an attempted one that fails: the surrounding elements do not enclose or point to the subject, form an awkward or incomplete shape, or compete with the subject for attention rather than guiding the eye toward it.
- **adequate** (4-6): A framing device is present and partially works—it loosely encloses or leads toward the subject—but the shape is imperfect, surrounds the subject only partially, feels somewhat arbitrary, or is mildly distracting or poorly integrated with the rest of the scene.
- **strong** (7-10): A clear, well-formed framing device encircles or encloses the subject, creates an appealing shape, integrates naturally into the environment, and unmistakably directs focus to the subject without drawing attention to itself. Cite the concrete framing element (e.g. a doorway, arch, or foreground silhouette) and how it isolates and elevates the subject.
- **not_applicable** (5) (not attempted / not applicable): Set `not_attempted` when the photograph makes no use of a framing device and is not composed around one—an open composition where enclosing the subject is simply not the operative technique.
